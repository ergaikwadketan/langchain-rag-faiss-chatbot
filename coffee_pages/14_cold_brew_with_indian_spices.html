Cold Brew with Indian Spices

Overview

Cold brew extracts coffee slowly at room temperature or in the fridge, yielding a smooth, low-acid base. Indian spices can be introduced via syrups or short infusions after brewing.

Ingredients (Base & Optional)

  • Coarsely ground coffee (1:8–1:10 coffee to water by weight)
  • Cold water
  • Spice syrup (cardamom-cinnamon-clove) optional
  • Milk/ice as desired

Brew 12–18 hours; strain thoroughly. Spices can be overpowering if steeped too long—add via syrup or brief infusion.

Method

  1. Combine grounds and water; steep covered 12–18 hours.
  2. Strain with a fine filter until clear.
  3. Sweeten or add spice syrup; serve over ice with or without milk.

Keep concentrate chilled; dilute 1:1 or to taste when serving.

Variations & Regional Twists

  • Jaggery-spice syrup for Indian dessert vibes.
  • Coconut cold brew for a tropical note.
  • Saffron-cardamom garnish for festivities.

Flavor Profile & Pairings

Smooth, chocolatey, rounded; spices add layered aroma without bitterness when used as syrups.

Benefits, Cautions & Practical Tips

  • Practical: make-ahead; consistent for crowds.
  • Caution: concentrates can be quite strong—dilute to preference.
  • Tip: large cube ice melts slower and preserves flavor.

Cultural & Historical Notes

Cold brew is global; Indian spice twists appeared as cafes explored seasonal menus and experimentation at home grew.

FAQ

Metallic taste?
Usually from over-extraction or poor water; adjust grind and time.
Spices in the brew or after?
After, via syrup, gives more control.
Shelf life?
3–5 days refrigerated for best flavor.
