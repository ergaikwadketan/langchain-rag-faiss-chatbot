Beaten Coffee (Phenti Hui Coffee)

Overview

Beaten coffee is a home-style method of whipping instant coffee and sugar into a thick, light-brown paste before adding hot milk. It became popular for its cafe-like foam without any machine.

Ingredients (Base & Optional)

  • 2 tsp instant coffee
  • 2 tsp sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tsp hot water
  • Hot milk

Whipping incorporates air; a small bowl and spoon work, but a mini whisk speeds things up.

Method

  1. Whisk instant coffee, sugar, and hot water vigorously until pale and fluffy (3–5 minutes).
  2. Pour hot milk into a cup and stir in a spoonful of the coffee whip.
  3. Top with more whip and gently swirl.

Make a jar of whip and refrigerate for a day for quick cups; stir before use.

Variations & Regional Twists

  • Iced beaten coffee: dissolve whip into cold milk over ice.
  • Spiced: add cardamom or cinnamon to the whip.
  • Mocha: whisk in cocoa for a chocolatey foam.

Flavor Profile & Pairings

Creamy and sweet with a pleasant bitter note from instant coffee; foam adds texture and visual appeal.

Benefits, Cautions & Practical Tips

  • Practical: no special equipment; quick cafe-style foam.
  • Caution: sweetness can creep up—adjust sugar to taste.
  • Tip: a few drops of vanilla make it dessert-like.

Cultural & Historical Notes

A nostalgic household technique across India, resurfacing in waves across decades, including the home-cafe trend era.

FAQ

Can I use ground coffee?
Instant coffee works best; grounds don’t whip.
How long does the whip keep?
Up to a day in the fridge; texture may loosen slightly.
Does it work with plant milks?
Yes—barista blends foam best.
