Cardamom Coffee (Elaichi Coffee)

Overview

Cardamom coffee highlights the floral, citrusy character of elaichi. A little goes a long way; freshly cracked pods deliver vivid aroma.

Ingredients (Base & Optional)

  • Freshly brewed coffee
  • 2–3 green cardamom pods, crushed
  • Milk and sweetener as desired

Bruise pods to expose seeds; avoid pre-ground powder if possible, or use very fresh powder.

Method

  1. Steep crushed pods in hot coffee for 2–3 minutes; strain.
  2. Add milk and sweetener to taste.

For a stronger cardamom presence, simmer pods in milk briefly before adding coffee.

Variations & Regional Twists

  • Rose-cardamom: add a drop of food-grade rose water.
  • Saffron-cardamom: festive and aromatic.
  • Iced: cool and serve over ice; garnish with a cracked pod.

Flavor Profile & Pairings

Fragrant, citrusy-floral, slightly mentholic; complements coffee’s roast without overwhelming it when used lightly.

Benefits, Cautions & Practical Tips

  • Practical: minimal ingredients; high aroma impact.
  • Caution: too much can taste medicinal; go light.
  • Tip: pair with lightly sweet biscuits or khari.

Cultural & Historical Notes

Cardamom is beloved across South Asia in sweets and beverages; elaichi chai is iconic, and coffee variants follow the same flavor logic.

FAQ

Pods or powder?
Pods keep aroma longer; powder is convenient but fades quickly.
Can I pre-infuse?
Yes—make a small syrup with cracked pods for repeatable strength.
Does dark roast work?
Yes—cardamom shines through robust profiles.
