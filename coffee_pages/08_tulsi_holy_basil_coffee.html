Tulsi (Holy Basil) Coffee

Overview

Tulsi (holy basil) lends a sweet, clove-like aroma to coffee. Use light touches to avoid overwhelming the cup.

Ingredients (Base & Optional)

  • Freshly brewed coffee
  • A few fresh tulsi leaves or a pinch of dried tulsi
  • Milk/sweetener optional

Fresh leaves are aromatic; rinse and bruise lightly before steeping.

Method

  1. Steep tulsi in hot coffee for 1–2 minutes; strain.
  2. Add milk/sweetener as preferred.

Tulsi’s character is intense; a brief infusion is usually enough.

Variations & Regional Twists

  • Tulsi-cardamom pairing for a floral cup.
  • Iced tulsi coffee with a touch of honey.
  • Coconut milk tulsi latte for a tropical vibe.

Flavor Profile & Pairings

Aromatic, slightly clove-like, clean finish; tulsi introduces a herbal lift over coffee’s base.

Benefits, Cautions & Practical Tips

  • Practical: garden-friendly herb; minimal effort.
  • Caution: flavor can dominate—use sparingly.
  • Tip: pair with mild sweets to let aroma shine.

Cultural & Historical Notes

Tulsi holds a special place in many Indian homes and courtyards; culinary uses vary by household preference.

FAQ

Fresh or dried?
Fresh offers livelier aroma; dried is convenient.
Can I blend tulsi powders?
Use tiny amounts; powders can taste harsh if overused.
Does it go with espresso?
Yes—use brief contact time to avoid bitterness stacking.
